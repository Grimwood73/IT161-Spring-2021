Social Media Optimization (SMO)

Social media networks have exploded in popularity in recent years with over 3.9 billion people using social media worldwide, and 82% of Americans now active on social media sites (Dean). For companies looking to broaden their audience and establish a web presence, it is more important than ever to use social media to its fullest potential. Optimizing a website and its content for sharing on social media to strategically promote a brand and generate website traffic is called Social Media Optimization, or SMO for short. Search Engine Optimization (SEO) has also become tightly intertwined with SMO, as search engines increasingly look to social media networks to rank their search results. In basic terms, the more shares a website has on social media the higher its search-engine ranking is likely to be (Rayson). However, SMO is more nuanced than just posting links to social media sites - by posting hundreds of unpopular, low-quality posts all over social media you run the risk of making your brand seem unpopular. Instead, focusing on a curated, high-quality social media presence will almost always garner the best results. Optimizing a website for social media can involve a wide variety of techniques, but here are a few tips for good SMO: Share Buttons - make it easy for users to share your content by adding convenient share buttons to the page. Social Login - make it easy for users to comment on your content using their social media login information. Shareable Content - use content like infographics or short and easily-readable articles that can be shared and digested quickly by social media users (Rayson). Adding these items to a website will increase how usable, visible, and shareable your content and brand are to social media users, ultimately increasing the brand’s presence and driving website traffic. While optimizing, it is important to note that using social media to try to directly sell a product or service tends to be an unsuccessful strategy. Social media users usually want to be entertained and/or educated, not sold to, so content that focuses on those goals will generally do better (Standberry). By implementing good SMO you can make a brand more recognizable, more recommended, and more trusted by your target audience.
